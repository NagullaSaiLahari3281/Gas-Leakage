Project Overview

The Automated LPG Leakage Detection and Gas Reduction System is developed to improve safety in homes and industries. The system continuously monitors gas levels and quickly detects leakage. When gas leakage occurs, the system alerts users and activates mechanisms to reduce gas concentration.

Gas Detection

An MQ-2 gas sensor is used to detect gases such as LPG, methane, propane, hydrogen, and smoke. The sensor constantly monitors the surrounding air and sends signals to the Arduino when gas levels increase.

Microcontroller Processing

Arduino UNO acts as the central controller of the system. It receives data from the sensor, processes the gas concentration level, and compares it with a predefined threshold value to detect gas leakage.

Alert System

When gas concentration exceeds the safe threshold level, the Arduino activates a buzzer to warn nearby people. This alert helps users identify a dangerous gas leakage situation quickly.

Gas Reduction Mechanism

An exhaust fan connected to a DC motor automatically turns on when gas leakage is detected. The fan helps remove leaked gas from the environment and improves ventilation.

Safety Benefits

This system helps prevent dangerous situations such as fire accidents, explosions, and health hazards caused by harmful gases. Early detection allows users to take quick action.

Applications

The system can be used in homes, kitchens, restaurants, laboratories, gas storage areas, and industries where LPG or combustible gases are commonly used.

Future Improvements

Future upgrades may include IoT integration for mobile notifications, GSM modules for SMS alerts, and LCD displays to show real-time gas concentration levels.
