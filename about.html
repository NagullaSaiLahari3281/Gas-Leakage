Project Overview

The Automated LPG Leakage Detection and Gas Reduction System is designed to enhance safety in homes and industrial environments. The system continuously monitors gas levels and quickly detects any leakage. When a leak is detected, the system automatically alerts users and activates safety mechanisms to reduce gas concentration.

Gas Detection

The system uses an MQ-2 gas sensor to detect gases such as LPG, methane, propane, hydrogen, and smoke. The sensor continuously monitors the surrounding air and sends analog signals to the Arduino when gas levels increase.

Microcontroller Processing

Arduino UNO acts as the main controller of the system. It reads the sensor data, processes the gas concentration levels, and compares them with a predefined threshold value to determine whether gas leakage has occurred.

Alert System

When the gas concentration exceeds the safe threshold level, the Arduino activates a buzzer to warn people nearby. This audible alert helps users quickly identify a dangerous gas leak situation.

Gas Reduction Mechanism

An exhaust fan connected to a DC motor automatically turns on when gas leakage is detected. The fan helps remove leaked gas from the surrounding environment and improves ventilation to reduce potential hazards.

Safety Benefits

This project helps prevent dangerous accidents such as fire outbreaks, explosions, and health problems caused by inhaling harmful gases. Early detection allows users to take action quickly and safely.

Applications

The system can be used in homes, kitchens, restaurants, laboratories, gas storage areas, and industries where LPG or combustible gases are commonly used.

Future Improvements

The system can be further improved by adding IoT technology to send alerts to mobile phones, integrating GSM modules for SMS alerts, or adding LCD displays to show real-time gas concentration levels.
